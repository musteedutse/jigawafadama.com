BACKGROUND

Jigawa State has secured a USD20 million financial commitment from the World Bank for the execution of the Nigeria COVID-19 Action Recovery and Economic Stimulus (NG-CARES) Programme. This multi-sectoral initiative aims to provide urgent relief to impoverished and vulnerable individuals, households, smallholder farmers, and SMEs negatively impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic over a two-year span (2021-2023). The program's execution will be spearheaded by individual states, capitalizing on existing state agencies as platforms, utilizing the Program for Result (PforR) financing instrument across three Results Areas with 11 Disbursement Linked Indicators for participating states. Concurrently, the Federal CARES Support Unit (FCSU) will furnish technical assistance to states. Jigawa State, as part of the NG-CARES Programme, has secured a USD20 million grant from the World Bank to address the immediate needs of poor and vulnerable individuals, households, smallholder farmers, and SMEs affected by the COVID-19 pandemic during the period 2021-2023. The state-driven initiative leverages existing state agencies, employing the Program for Result (PforR) financing instrument and focusing on three Results Areas with 11 Disbursement Linked Indicators for participating states. The Federal CARES Support Unit (FCSU) will extend technical support to states.

The NG-CARES Programme, operating under the World Bank's Program for Results (PforR) financing mechanism, serves as a comprehensive intervention targeting immediate emergency relief for those adversely affected by the COVID-19 pandemic. Spanning 2021-2023, this two-year budget support operation prioritizes the fulfillment of three key objectives outlined in the Federal Government's Economic Sustainability Plan (ESP). At the state level, the NG-CARES will work towards expanding social assistance interventions, utilizing Labor Intensive Public Works (LIPW) in the social and agriculture sectors, and fostering economic stimulation by supporting agricultural value chains, preventing the collapse of informal enterprises, and enhancing technology to overcome COVID-19-induced constraints. The overarching development objective of NG-CARES is to "expand access to livelihood support and food security services, and grants for poor and vulnerable households and firms." Key performance indicators include the number of beneficiaries of targeted safety nets with access to basic social services (disaggregated by gender), the number of farmers supported to increase food production (disaggregated by gender), and the number of vulnerable and viable firms supported by the Programme (disaggregated by gender).

OVERVIEW OF J-CARES PROGRAM

The World Bank's support to the Federal Government of Nigeria (FGN) towards mitigating the impacts of the COVID-19-induced economic crisis, The NG-CARES programme is designed to restore the livelihoods of the poor and vulnerable, maintaining food security, and facilitating recovery of MSEs are some of the key objectives set out in the Governmentâ€™s Economic Sustainability Plan (ESP) to respond to the current economic crisis in Nigeria. The Jigawa state government is among the benefiting states of Federal Government grant to support citizens affected by COVID-19 pandemic. The Nigerian COVID-19 Action Recovery and Economic Stimulus NG-CARES programs the coordinating body of CARES programs across the focal states. Hence the Jigawa COVID-19 Action Recovery and Economic Stimulus (J-CARES) program came into force. The Fadama structure is coordinating the food security and livelihood components of the programme under result area II, the Programme Development Objective (PDO) is to protect the livelihood and food security of poor and vulnerable families and facilitate the recovery of local economic activity in the state. Following the commencement of the program implementation, the government observed cross-implementation inefficiency in the selection of target beneficiaries, hence inaugurated this committee charged with responsibility of equitable and transparent section of the beneficiaries. The Fadama project is responsible for coordinating the implementation of result area II Increasing food Security and safe functioning of food supply chains poor households, comprising of four Disbursement Link indicators (DLIs). The Jigawa State Government has selected two (2) DLIs to be implemented under Result Area II (Fadama), namely: DLI 2.1 Agricultural input and Services (seeds, fertilizers, day old chicks, fingerlings and agrochemicals) and DLI 2.3: Agricultural Assets (Water pumps and processing machine, sheep and goat, Day old Chicks).
